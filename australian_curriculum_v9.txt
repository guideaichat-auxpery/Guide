AUSTRALIAN CURRICULUM V9 INTEGRATION
====================================

ENGLISH CURRICULUM STRUCTURE:
Three interrelated strands:
- Language: How English works for interaction, text structure, expressing ideas
- Literature: Personal, cultural, social and aesthetic value texts
- Literacy: Creating meaning through application of language skills

Key Focus Areas:
- Foundation to Year 10 progression
- Reading, viewing, listening, speaking, writing, creating
- Critical and creative thinking
- Intercultural understanding
- First Nations Australian perspectives

HUMANITIES AND SOCIAL SCIENCES (HASS):
Integrated approach for F-6, then specialised subjects 7-10

HASS F-6 Strands:
- Knowledge and Understanding (History, Geography, Civics & Citizenship, Economics & Business)
- Skills (Questioning/researching, Interpreting/analysing, Concluding/decision-making, Communicating)

HASS Concepts:
- Significance, Continuity and change, Cause and effect
- Place and space, Interconnections
- Identity and diversity, Democracy and citizenship
- Resource allocation and making choices

CIVICS AND CITIZENSHIP (Years 7-10):
Government and Democracy:
- Australian federal system, Constitution, responsible government, federalism
- Democratic characteristics: freedom of speech, association, assembly, religion, movement
- Active citizenship and participation

Laws and Citizens:
- Australian legal system, rule of law, court system
- Justice principles, equality, fair trial rights
- Access to justice and legal representation

Citizenship, Diversity and Identity:
- Secular democracy, multi-faith society, cultural diversity
- Christian heritage, First Nations histories, migrant communities
- Values: freedom, respect, fairness, equality of opportunity

ECONOMICS AND BUSINESS (Years 7-10):
Core Concepts:
- Resource allocation and decision-making (scarcity, opportunity cost)
- Business environment (production, exchange, competitive advantage)
- Entrepreneurship (innovation, risk-taking, leadership)
- Work and work futures (contribution to wellbeing, rights/responsibilities)
- Consumer and financial literacy (informed decision-making)

Year 7 Focus: "Individuals, businesses and entrepreneurs"
- Decision-making for resource allocation
- Types of businesses and entrepreneurial characteristics
- Work types and income derivation
- Consumer and business rights/responsibilities

GEOGRAPHY (Years 7-10):
Core Concepts:
- Place (characteristics, influence on outcomes)
- Space (location, distance, patterns, organisation)
- Environment (significance, interactions, sustainability)
- Scale (personal to global investigation levels)
- Change (causes, temporal understanding, future thinking)
- Interconnections (relationships, interactions, interdependencies)
- Sustainability (environmental, economic, social capacity)

Year 7 Topics:
- Water in the world (distribution, scarcity, management, hazards)
- Place and liveability (factors, services, planning, sustainability)

HISTORY (Years 7-10):
Structure:
- Knowledge and Understanding (civilisations, societies, events)
- Skills (Questioning, using sources, perspectives/interpretations, communicating)

Core Concepts:
- Evidence (primary/secondary source interpretation)
- Perspectives (viewpoints shaped by beliefs, values, experiences)
- Interpretations and contestability (debate, new evidence)
- Continuity and change (what remains/changes over time)
- Cause and effect (short/long-term, intended/unintended)
- Significance (importance over time and place)

Year 7 Focus:
- Deep time history of Australia (First Nations Peoples, earliest societies)
- Ancient world (Greece, Rome, Egypt, India, China)

GENERAL CAPABILITIES INTEGRATED ACROSS ALL AREAS:
- Literacy and Numeracy
- Information and Communication Technology
- Critical and Creative Thinking
- Personal and Social Capability
- Ethical Understanding
- Intercultural Understanding

CROSS-CURRICULUM PRIORITIES:
- Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Histories and Cultures
- Asia and Australia's Engagement with Asia
- Sustainability

ASSESSMENT APPROACHES:
- Achievement standards for each year level
- Content descriptions with elaborations
- Inquiry-based learning through questions
- Evidence-based evaluation and reflection
- Portfolio and performance-based assessment

KEY TERMINOLOGY AND GLOSSARY INTEGRATION:
English: Academic texts, evaluative language, multimodal texts, literary devices
HASS: Significance, interconnections, sustainability, diversity, democracy
Geography: Liveability, environmental resources, spatial distribution
History: Primary/secondary sources, perspectives, continuity/change
Civics: Active citizenship, rule of law, constitutional monarchy
Economics: Opportunity cost, entrepreneurship, resource allocation

This framework ensures authentic curriculum alignment while maintaining flexibility for diverse learning contexts and student needs.
