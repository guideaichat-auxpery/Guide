AUSTRALIAN CURRICULUM V9: GENERAL CAPABILITIES

=== LITERACY ===

Framework - 3 Elements:

SPEAKING AND LISTENING:
Sub-elements: Listening, Interacting, Speaking
- Listening: Building meaning from spoken/audio texts with active listening processes
- Interacting: Two-way interaction processes to clarify and create understanding in curriculum contexts
- Speaking: Selecting language and using voice to express ideas appropriate to audience, purpose and task

READING AND VIEWING:
Sub-elements: Phonological awareness, Phonic knowledge and word recognition, Fluency, Understanding texts
- Phonological awareness: Working with individual sounds (phonemes) in spoken words
- Phonic knowledge: Using letter-sound relationships to decode and spell words
- Fluency: Reading with speed, accuracy, expression and comprehension
- Understanding texts: Decoding, analysing and evaluating increasingly complex texts

WRITING:
Sub-elements: Creating texts, Grammar, Punctuation, Spelling, Handwriting and keyboarding
- Creating texts: Composing for different purposes and audiences
- Grammar: Understanding language structures and patterns
- Punctuation: Using conventions to enhance meaning and readability
- Spelling: Applying patterns and rules for accurate word construction
- Handwriting/keyboarding: Producing legible text efficiently

=== NUMERACY ===

Framework - 3 Elements:

NUMBER SENSE AND ALGEBRA:
Sub-elements: Number and place value, Counting processes, Additive strategies, Multiplicative strategies, Interpreting fractions, Proportional thinking, Number patterns and algebraic thinking, Understanding money

MEASUREMENT AND GEOMETRY:
Sub-elements: Understanding units of measurement, Understanding geometric properties, Positioning and locating, Measuring time

STATISTICS AND PROBABILITY:
Sub-elements: Understanding chance, Interpreting and representing data

=== DIGITAL LITERACY ===

Framework - 4 Elements:

PRACTISING DIGITAL SAFETY AND WELLBEING:
Sub-elements: Manage online safety, Manage digital privacy and identity, Manage digital wellbeing

INVESTIGATING:
Sub-elements: Locate information, Acquire and collate data, Interpret data

CREATING AND EXCHANGING:
Sub-elements: Plan, Create/communicate/collaborate, Respect intellectual property

MANAGING AND OPERATING:
Sub-elements: Manage content, Protect content, Select and operate tools

=== INTERCULTURAL UNDERSTANDING ===

Framework - 3 Elements:

REFLECTING ON CULTURE AND CULTURAL DIVERSITY:
Sub-elements: Reflect on relationship between cultures and identities, Examine cultural perspectives and world views, Explore influence of cultures on interactions

ENGAGING WITH CULTURAL AND LINGUISTIC DIVERSITY:
Sub-elements: Communicate responsively, Develop multiple perspectives, Develop empathy

NAVIGATING INTERCULTURAL CONTEXTS:
Sub-elements: Consider responses to intercultural experiences, Respond to biases/stereotypes/prejudices/discrimination, Adapt in intercultural exchanges

=== ETHICAL UNDERSTANDING ===

Framework - 2 Elements:

UNDERSTANDING ETHICAL CONCEPTS AND PERSPECTIVES:
Sub-elements: Explore ethical concepts, Examine values/rights/responsibilities/ethical norms, Recognise influences on ethical behaviour and perspectives

RESPONDING TO ETHICAL ISSUES:
Sub-elements: Explore ethical perspectives and frameworks, Explore ethical issues, Make and reflect on ethical decisions

Key Terms Across All Capabilities:
- bias: Presentation of one perspective, favouring one side in an argument
- communication: Giving, receiving and responding to information, knowledge, understanding, processes, skills, opinions, beliefs, perspectives, needs and emotions
- community: A group of people linked by a common social structure and sense of belonging
- context: The situation or setting of an event, problem or application, whether real or constructed
- empathy: The ability to understand and share the feelings of another person
- respect: Behaviour that acknowledges and takes account of the rights, needs, wishes and feelings of others
- values: Shared ideas and beliefs specific to individuals and groups
- world views: Sets of attitudes, values and beliefs about the world around us that inform our thoughts and actions
