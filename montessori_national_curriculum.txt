MONTESSORI NATIONAL CURRICULUM 2011
===================================

OVERVIEW:
The Montessori National Curriculum provides a comprehensive framework for children from birth to eighteen years, organised according to the three planes of child and adolescent development:

FIRST PLANE (Birth to 6 years): Early Years Learning Programme
- Absorbent mind period
- Development of movement, language, senses
- Fundamental life skills
- Mathematics, science, geography and history foundations

SECOND PLANE (6 to 12 years): Primary School Education  
- Reasoning mind period
- Cosmic Education approach
- Great Stories as foundation
- Interconnected learning across all subjects

THIRD PLANE (12 to 18 years): Secondary School Education
- Social orientation and creative power
- Real-world learning and community engagement
- Personal qualities and competencies development

HUMAN TENDENCIES:
- Exploration and orientation
- Communication and abstraction
- Work and activity
- Repetition and concentration
- Self-perfection and independence

PREPARED ENVIRONMENT PRINCIPLES:
- Order and beauty
- Mixed age groupings
- Freedom within limits
- Hands-on learning materials
- Extended work cycles
- Natural materials and real tools

COSMIC EDUCATION PHILOSOPHY:
The universe story as foundation for all learning, showing interconnections between:
- Historical development
- Ecological systems  
- Social structures
- Economic relationships
- Scientific principles
- Cultural expressions

CURRICULUM AREAS:
1. Practical Life
2. Sensorial Education
3. Language
4. Mathematics
5. Cultural Studies (History, Geography, Science, Arts)

DEVELOPMENTAL CHARACTERISTICS BY PLANE:
First Plane (0-6): Absorbent mind, sensory exploration, concrete learning
Second Plane (6-12): Reasoning mind, imagination, moral development
Third Plane (12-18): Social consciousness, identity formation, preparation for adult life

ASSESSMENT APPROACH:
- Observation-based assessment
- Portfolio documentation
- Self-assessment and reflection
- Focus on growth and development rather than comparison
- Recognition of individual learning pathways

This curriculum emphasises the child's natural development, intrinsic motivation, and preparation for life as a contributing member of society.
