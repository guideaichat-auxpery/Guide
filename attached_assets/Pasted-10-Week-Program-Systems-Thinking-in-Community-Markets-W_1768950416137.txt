10-Week Program: Systems Thinking in Community Markets
Week 1: Introduction to Markets and Systems Thinking
Lesson 1: What is a market? Explore different types of markets worldwide (e.g., farmers' markets, flea markets, Artists markets).
Activities: Group brainstorming on what makes a successful market.
Lesson 2: Introduction to systems thinking.
Activities: Visual mapping exercise where students identify components of a community market (stakeholders, resources/infrastructure, products).
Outcomes: Create a mind map of their ideas around community engagement and market benefits.

Week 2: Stakeholder Engagement
Lesson 3: Who are the stakeholders in a market? Discuss roles (vendors, customers, local government, etc.).
Activities: Role-play activity where students act out stakeholder meetings to explore perspectives.
Lesson 4: Engaging stakeholders effectively.
Activities: Create simple presentations summarising key stakeholders and how to communicate with them.

Week 3: Design Thinking for Markets
Lesson 5: Basics of design thinking. What is user-centric design?
Activities: Workshop where students sketch ideas for market layouts and booths based on user experience.
Lesson 6: Feedback loops — gathering information from potential users.
Activities: Conduct surveys or interviews with peers about their ideal market experience.

Week 4: Marketing Fundamentals
Lesson 7: Introduction to marketing concepts. Branding and visual identity.
Activities: Students design logos and promotional materials for their ideal market stalls.
Lesson 8: Developing marketing strategies tailored for different audiences.
Activities: Groups develop taglines or slogans for their market concepts, sharing them in a mini-pitch format.

Week 5: Understanding Community Needs
Lesson 9: What does our community need? Explore cultural, social, and economic aspects that can be addressed through a market.
Activities: Research project where students investigate local needs and how markets can meet them (focus on local context).
Lesson 10: Present findings on community needs to the class.

Week 6: Sustainability and Ethics in Markets
Lesson 11: Discussion on sustainability practices in markets (waste management, sourcing locally).
Activities: Create an action plan outlining sustainable practices that could be implemented at the Twilight Market.
Lesson 12: Explore ethical considerations and social responsibility within businesses.

Week 7: Logistics Planning
Lesson 13: Practical considerations of running a market (budgeting, permits, site logistics).
Activities: Simulation game where students must budget for their stalls while considering expenses and income potential!
Lesson 14: Risk assessment — identifying potential challenges/risks associated with running the market.

Week 8: Social Systems and Community Engagement
Lesson 15: Discuss the role of social systems in communities (how they interact and support each other).
Lesson 16: Engage with community leaders or business owners as guest speakers to discuss their experiences with markets.

Week 9: Final Preparations for Twilight Market
Lesson 17: Revisit all learned concepts to prepare for the upcoming Marketplace.
Activities: Finalize marketing materials, layout designs, stall ideas, etc.
Lesson 18: Present final plans for their part in the Twilight Market as teams. Provide peer feedback.

Week 10: The Twilight Market!
Organise a full-scale mock-up of the Twilight Market where Year Sevens run food stalls, Year Nines operate business stalls, and your three focus students manage the overall experience based on their learnings throughout the term.
Post-event reflection sessions where everyone shares successes, challenges faced, and lessons learned.
Supporting Students with High Literacy Needs
Use visuals extensively—diagrams, infographics, photos—to convey information clearly.
Encourage group work so that students can collaborate and support each other in completing tasks.
Provide templates for presentations or reports to help scaffold writing tasks.
Use multi-modal resources such as videos or podcasts to introduce concepts before deeper discussions.
Follow-Up Questions for Reflection:
How did our understanding of systems thinking change how we approach organising events like this?
What was something surprising you learned about your community's needs?
How would you apply these skills in future projects or real-life situations?
